Raja Ampat

Raja Ampat, located off the northwest tip of Bird's Head Peninsula on the island of New Guinea in Indonesia's West Papua province, is an archipelago comprising over 1,500 small islands, cays, and shoals surrounding the four main islands of Misool, Salawati, Batanta, and Waigeo. This remote and pristine marine paradise is renowned for its extraordinary biodiversity, stunning coral reefs, and crystal-clear waters, making it one of the top diving destinations in the world. Raja Ampat's underwater landscape is teeming with a dazzling array of marine life, including vibrant coral gardens, reef sharks, manta rays, and countless species of tropical fish. Above the water, the islands boast breathtaking scenery, with lush forests, limestone karsts, and secluded beaches waiting to be explored.

The Raja Ampat Islands offer unparalleled opportunities for diving and snorkeling amidst some of the richest marine biodiversity on the planet. From colorful coral reefs to underwater caves and drop-offs, the underwater landscapes of Raja Ampat are a paradise for underwater enthusiasts.

The iconic Wayag Islands in Raja Ampat are famous for their breathtaking panoramic views, towering limestone karsts, and turquoise lagoons. Visitors can hike to the top of the karsts for a bird's-eye view of the surrounding islands and crystal-clear waters below, making it a highlight of any trip to Raja Ampat.
