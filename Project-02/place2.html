Labuan Bajo

Labuan Bajo, located on the western tip of Flores Island in Indonesia, serves as the gateway to the stunning Komodo National Park. This charming fishing town is a popular starting point for adventures to see the famous Komodo dragons, as well as exploring the region's pristine beaches, vibrant coral reefs, and picturesque islands. Labuan Bajo offers visitors a blend of natural beauty and exciting activities, from diving and snorkeling to hiking and wildlife spotting. The town itself has a range of accommodations, restaurants, and tour services, making it a convenient and attractive destination for travelers.

Padar Island, part of the Komodo National Park, is renowned for its dramatic landscapes and stunning viewpoints. The hike to the top of Padar Island offers breathtaking panoramic views, making it a must-visit for nature lovers and photographers.

Pink Beach, located on Komodo Island, is one of only a few pink sand beaches in the world. The unique color of the sand comes from the mixture of white sand and red coral fragments. This beautiful beach is a popular spot for snorkeling and diving, offering clear waters and vibrant marine life. The serene and picturesque setting of Pink Beach makes it a favorite among visitors to Komodo National Park.
